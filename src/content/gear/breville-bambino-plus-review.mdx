The Bambino Plus has been the default recommendation for home cappuccino for several years. The reasons are simple: it heats up in three seconds, it has a real steam wand, it fits under most cabinets, and it costs $499. At that price point, with those specs, the competition is thin.

This review is specifically about cappuccino. If you're primarily making lattes or Americanos, other machines compete more closely. Cappuccino requires textured milk, which requires a steam wand with real pressure — and that's where the Bambino Plus earns its recommendation.

## The steam wand

The wand is a single-hole steam tip, which is the constraint on this machine. Single-hole wands require more technique than multi-hole commercial wands — they move through the milk more slowly, which means you have more time in the air-introduction phase and less room for error.

The upside: if you learn on a single-hole wand, you develop better feel for the milk. The technique transfers to any machine. The downside: your first 50 cappuccinos will probably have lumpy foam.

Steam pressure is adequate but not exceptional. You can texture a 150ml pitcher correctly, but it takes more active management than a $1,500 machine. This is the honest version of "good for the price."

## The 3-second heat-up

This matters more than the spec sheet suggests. The practical implication is that you can pull an espresso, decide to make a second one, and not wait. In a real kitchen morning, this eliminates the friction that turns "I'll make a proper coffee" into "I'll just use the capsule machine."

The auto-purge function — which briefly opens the steam valve after you switch from steam mode — keeps the boiler temperature correct for espresso without manual intervention. It's a small thing done well.

## What it doesn't do

The single boiler means you wait. Pulling a shot and then steaming requires a 45-second temperature transition. For one cappuccino this is fine. For two in sequence, it adds up.

The steam wand doesn't pivot as freely as commercial wands, which creates some awkward pitcher angles when you're learning. You adapt.

## The verdict

**Cappuccino Rating: 8/10.** The Bambino Plus is a capable machine that teaches real technique without punishing you financially. If you're coming from capsules or a super-automatic, it will produce noticeably better cappuccino within two weeks of practice. If you already own a machine with a commercial steam wand, there's no reason to step down.

The comparison worth knowing: the Rancilio Silvia at $799 has a larger boiler and a more capable steam wand. If your budget extends there and you care enough to ask, buy the Silvia.
